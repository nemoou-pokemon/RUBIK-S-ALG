The First Step

What's a Cross?

The Cross is the first step of the CFOP method and involves solving four edge pieces of the same color to form a plus sign (or "cross") on one face, typically the white face. Each edge must also be aligned with the correct center piece on adjacent sides, not just the white center. This means you’re not just matching colors on one face, but also making sure each edge is correctly positioned. While beginners may place edges one by one, speedcubers plan and solve the cross efficiently during inspection, often in under 8 moves. Mastering the cross is essential for a fast solve.

What's a Daisy Cross?

The Daisy Cross is a simplified method for beginners to build the white cross on the Rubik’s Cube. Instead of placing white edge pieces directly onto the white face, you first create a "flower" shape on the yellow face—this means surrounding the yellow center with four white edge pieces. It looks like a daisy, hence the name. Once the daisy is complete, you find the side color of each white edge (for example, if the piece is white-red, look for the red center). Then, rotate the entire face so the side color of that white edge matches its center. After it's aligned, you rotate that face 180 degrees to move the white edge down to the white face, placing it correctly in the cross. Repeat this process for the remaining three edges. By the end, you'll have a complete white cross with each white edge correctly aligned with the adjacent center color. This method helps beginners focus on piece positioning without worrying about orientation or complex moves. It’s slower than the standard CFOP cross but very effective for learning.
